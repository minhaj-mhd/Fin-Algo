EARLY-EXIT overlay (exit/risk study) — exploratory, no verdict authority
Script: scripts/analysis/wf_early_exit.py
Run: 2026-06-11 | 6 folds WF OOS 2024-09 -> 2026-06 | 11,692 candidates | cost 10 bps
Models: v3_15min_clean (15m), v10_native_1h (1h) | retrained per fold (purged WF)

IDEA: after entering a 1h top-3 trade, watch the 15m own-side conviction rank during
the hold; exit early on decay to cut losses. Cost identical either way (one round-trip),
so it's a pure timing overlay. Checkpoints after sub-periods 1/2/3 (dt1+75/90/105);
exit realizes the compounded return so far. No look-ahead (rank + return-so-far only).

Policies: full_hold | conv_level (exit if rank<0.5) | conv_momentum (exit if rank falls)
          | price_stop (control: exit at first checkpoint where signed return-so-far < 0)

VERDICT (exploratory):
- 15m conviction-exit genuinely CUTS LOSSES on bad trades: long losers -40.3->-23.0,
  short losers -56.5->-31.2. And it BEATS the dumb price-stop on mean, WR, median, and
  loser-cutting -> the 15m model adds real EXIT information beyond "bail when already red".
- On LONG, conv_level raises overall mean_net above full_hold (-7.5 vs -8.0) and improves
  median (-6.3 vs -9.4): a small genuine win by the script's own criterion.
- On SHORT, conv_level lowers mean (-9.3 vs -8.2) despite better WR/median = winner-clipping
  tax (exiting early also bails trades that would recover). conv_momentum cuts short losers best.
- BUT all policies remain net-NEGATIVE: this is loss-MITIGATION on a losing host, not profit.
  The 1h host is net-negative, so trimming losses can't cross zero on its own.

TAKEAWAY: 15m conviction is weak for ENTRY but genuinely useful as an EXIT/RISK overlay.
Pair with a profitable entry source (new-information levers) to make it a real exit module.
Recommended default policy: conv_level (longs); revisit winner-clipping on shorts.
